Co-Living

Each Embassy location will offer short and long term residential accommodations in a co-living, community setting. Long term residents live in the Embassy as their primary residence, provide an anchor to local culture, and are active participants in creating activities and events at their location.

Flexible Duration

In addition to long term accommodations, each Embassy provides short term accommodations operated much like a B&B. Members reserve space at any location in the network, anywhere around the world, as needed to support their travel, work, and collaborations.

Community Space

Every Embassy location includes a community space open to members and friends to gather and connect. These spaces differ per-location. From co-working space to restaurants, each community space is a key tie-in to local culture and a way to remain outward focused in ideation, problem-solving, and relationship-building.

A service-based, purpose-driven organization

The Embassy Network is primarily a coordinating body that connects properties, coordinates membership, and provides online resources to keep the community connected.

Each individual property, or local embassy node, provides a curated, full-time space for organic and impromptu interactions. Each node has independent day-to-day operations and a distinct culture, hosting its own microcosm of workshops, salons, dinners, and other events. Residents are chosen by other residents, and the balance of rooms set aside for short- and long-term accommodation decided together between residents and the Global Embassy Network.

Members may either be full-time residents at a specific location in the network, or use the flexible accommodations much like they would use a Bed and Breakfast: as a replacement for the anonymous hotels, mass-produced food, and cultural disconnect that so often comes with traveling for work.

By leveraging co-location, the fresh perspectives that comes with travel, the organic interactions that happen in the home environment, and the power of food for building community, we feel that the investment that we and others make in ourselves can be vastly enhanced. Need a little time to focus and reflect? Don't worry, Embassy location residences and flexible accommodations all have space to get away.

Travel and always be at home

The first Embassy nodes are targeted for San Francisco and DC: two urban centers with high concentrations of creative individuals, both resident and transient. We consider our "minimum viable product" to be at least two cities for testing the financial and membership model.

Each Embassy location involves 3 specific functions:

  1. long-term residential rooms and/or suites,
  2. shorter-term accommodations,
  3. a public, possibly commercial, community-oriented space.
The archetypal design would have this community space on the ground floor, open to foot traffic, supporting collaborations and human interactions, and keeping a fresh flow of people and ideas through the space. Each location will decide the specific theme or purpose of this community space, as a function of its environment, local needs and culture. It might be a co-working space, cafe or restaurant, bar, or something else. The second floor would house short-term accommodation rooms, as well as a large, industrial-grade, shared kitchen, common rooms, and library. Finally, the third floor would contain the longer term full time residences. These are either to be small suites, large rooms, or a combination of both.

Community Supported

Passion and drive are cultivated by a supportive external environment, and every Embassy location is a safe haven for new ideas, hard work, reflection, and fun. Membership in the Embassy Network is exclusive-- we want only those willing to assert, with no apologies, their passion for innovation, experimentation, and changing the world for the better. If you see yourself in these words, then you are home. There is no right or wrong here. Only a shared purpose and committment.

Membership levels exist to suit different needs, and include primary-residence, blocks of per-nite accomodation, and organizational membership.

Be willing to surround yourself with excellence.

Our financial model is based on the following sources:

  • Partnering with community houses to create a distributed pool of accommodations for world changers.
  • Property management agreements with institutional partners
  • Booking or membership fees to support operations and tools for houses and members.

The Embassy Network will be operated as a community-profit organization (not-for-personal-profit). All profits will be re-invested into Embassy Network activities, real estate purchases, or community funding to support projects of our members.

The Embassy is a collaborative project made possible through community partnerships and you.

Our goal is to facilitate and promote a lifestyle built around organic creativity and innovation.
